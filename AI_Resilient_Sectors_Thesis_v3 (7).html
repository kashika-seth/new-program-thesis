New Program Thesis 2027

Why These Sectors — Setting the Context

Why physical, regulated, employer-linked programs are structurally differentiated — and why now

Core planning lens: A student entering in AY27 (Batch 2027–28) graduates in 2031. Curriculum decisions made today must be grounded in what roles will persist in 2030, not just what exists now. AI will automate tasks within roles — the 2030 graduate needs to work alongside AI: interpreting its output, taking physical action, holding legal accountability, and building human trust. These are the things AI structurally cannot do.
Changing Employment Landscape — India 2026–31
WEF Future of Jobs Report 2025: Healthcare support, logistics/supply chain, and green economy among the fastest-growing job categories globally. Supply-side training is the only way to meet this demand.
LinkedIn Jobs on the Rise India 2024: Healthcare Support in top 5 fastest-growing categories by hiring velocity. Supply Chain & Logistics and Aviation Operations in top 20.
MoHFW Health Workforce 2030: 4.3Mn nurse shortage and 13L+ AHP gap. Private training institutions are the only pipeline.
Urban Company IPO filings: average gross earnings ₹50,000+/month for active service professionals (electricians, plumbers, beauticians); top 20% earn ₹77,000/month — significantly above entry-level white collar
India's gig workforce: 7.7M in 2021, projected 23.5M by 2029 (NITI Aayog); delivery roles (₹25–40K/month with incentives) now compete with entry-level white collar — reflecting changing status of organised physical work
Lenskart model: Asia's largest eyewear chain with 1,500+ stores, 10,000+ employees; optometrists earn ₹22,000–39,500/month (Glassdoor, 983 salaries) — BSc Optometry graduates are the core hire at every store
Why Regulated Physical Professions Are the Moat
Physical patient contact, site supervision, flight operations — impossible to do remotely or digitally
Regulatory licences (INC, BCI, PCI, MoHFW, DGCA) are issued to humans — AI cannot legally hold them, creating a structural practitioner moat
New AI diagnostic/legal tools create new roles supporting AI, not replaced by it — "AI-imaging support technician" by 2028
Degree vs short course: degrees build licensing eligibility. Short courses are top-ups. For regulated professions, the degree is non-negotiable.
Sectors prioritised: large existing enrolment base (>1L/yr), fast regulatory approval (<9 months), immediate employer demand

Sector Evaluation & Selection

How we went from 50+ program categories to 8 AY27 priorities — and how each sector scores

We evaluated every credentialised program category in India against three questions. Sectors that cleared all three became the AY27 shortlist. Sectors that cleared one or two became the AY29+ pipeline.
1 — Demand & Market
Is there durable,
growing demand?
→ Annual national enrolment >50,000/yr
→ Sector CAGR >8%
→ Employer demand confirmed (active hiring)
→ Market size >₹10,000Cr addressable
→ Jobs growth structural, not cyclical
2 — AI Durability
Does the profession
survive automation?
→ Physical presence with patient/site required
→ Regulatory licence issued only to humans (INC / BCI / PCI / NCAHP / DGCA)
→ Real-time judgment in non-scripted situations
→ Human trust — patient/court won't accept AI
AI-Proof Score: weighted composite. Physical presence 30% + Licensing 25% + Judgment 20% + Trust 15% + Dexterity 10%.
3 — Regulatory & Launch Ease
How easy is
the launch?
→ Regulatory landscape: is the licensing body active, clear-cut & approachable? (AICTE = fast & clear; BCI = expensive upfront; INC = slow & political; NCAHP = new but functional)
→ Approval timeline: licence in hand within 12 months of application
→ Total cost (regulatory + infra) under ₹1.5Cr all-in
→ Admission route: counselling-based (state quota fills predictably) or direct merit (requires outreach but faster cycle)
Viability Score: regulatory fees (30%) + approval timeline (25%) + infra cost (20%) + regulatory landscape complexity (15%) + admission ease (10%).

Why Allied Health Is a "Grey Area" Regulation — and Why That's an Advantage
What Makes It Easier Than Nursing/Law
NCAHP Act 2021 is recent — full implementation still in progress, state councils not yet fully formed in all states
Unlike Nursing (INC) or Law (BCI), no single centralised inspection body — approval primarily through university Academic Council + State Health Dept
Early movers benefit from current lighter process before NCAHP tightens further
NCAHP now notifying curricula — provides clarity; institutions adopting early signal quality
Regulatory Risk to Watch
NCAHP becoming more active in 2026 — rules tightening over next 3 years
NEET requirement from AY26-27 will reduce intake volume initially vs non-NEET entry
State Allied Health Councils being formed — adds a new approval layer in some states
Hospital MoU is non-negotiable and has a 2–4 week sign-off cycle — start this first
Step-by-Step Approval Process — New AHP Program at Private University
Step 1 — Internal University Approval Month 1–2
Academic Council approves program. Board of Studies constituted. NCAHP notified curriculum adopted (mandatory). Faculty identification begins.
Step 2 — Hospital / Diagnostic Centre MoU Start here — binding constraint
Secure clinical partner MoU per specialisation. Radiology: imaging centre / hospital radiology dept. MLT: pathology lab. OT: hospital operating theatre. Typical sign-off: 2–4 weeks. Setup cost per specialisation: ₹15–80L for lab equipment.
Step 3 — State Allied Health Council / Health Dept Month 3–4
Formal intimation with faculty list, infrastructure documents, hospital MoU. In Rajasthan: State Health Dept (state council not fully formed). Response: 4–8 weeks typically.
Step 4 — NCAHP Institutional Registration Month 4–6
Register on NCAHP portal. Submit curricula alignment, faculty credentials, infrastructure self-declaration. NCAHP inspection process formalising in 2026.
Step 5 — NEET Admission Setup Month 5–6 · New from AY26-27
For NEET-mandatory programs: establish state counselling tie-up or deemed university quota. Confirm per-program NEET requirement with NCAHP directly.
Step 6 — First Admission Batch Month 6–9 · Batch 2026–27
Launch admissions. Recommended Year 1 intake: 30–60 seats per specialisation (lab + hospital clinical slots are the binding constraints, not regulatory caps). No NCAHP-prescribed intake cap.
Total timeline: 6–9 months from decision to first admission. Faster than Nursing (12–18 months for INC) and Law (12 months for BCI). Hospital MoU is the single binding constraint — start this before anything else.
